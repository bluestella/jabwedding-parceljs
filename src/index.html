Frequently Asked Questions

Ceremony

What time should I arrive at the church?

Please arrive at least 30 minutes before the ceremony (by 3:30 PM) to allow time for seating and to ensure the ceremony can begin promptly at 4:00 PM.

How long will the Catholic ceremony last?

The full Catholic wedding mass will last approximately 1.5 hours. This includes the liturgy, exchange of vows, and Holy Communion.

Will there be Filipino traditions?

Yes, we will be incorporating several Filipino wedding traditions including the veil, cord, and coin (arrhae) ceremonies during the mass.

Reception

How do I get from the church to the reception venue?

The reception venue is approximately 20 minutes from the church. We will provide shuttle service for guests without transportation. Shuttles will depart 15 minutes after the ceremony ends.

What time does the reception start?

The reception will begin at 6:30 PM with cocktails, followed by dinner at 7:30 PM. The program and celebration will continue until 11:00 PM.

Dress Code & General Information

What is the dress code?

The dress code is formal attire. For men, barong tagalog or formal suits are appropriate. For women, formal dresses or Filipiniana attire is recommended. Please avoid wearing white or ivory, as these colors are reserved for the bride.

Can I bring a plus-one/date?

Only guests specifically named on the invitation are included in our guest count. Please refer to your invitation for details, and contact us if you have questions.

Accommodations & Special Requests

Will food be accommodating of dietary restrictions?

Yes, we will offer vegetarian and gluten-free options. Please indicate any dietary restrictions when you RSVP so we can make arrangements.

Are there hotels near the venue?

Yes, we have arrangements with Hillcreek Gardens for discounted room rates. Please mention our wedding when booking. See our "Travel & Stay" section for more details.

Filipino Wedding Traditions

Our ceremony will incorporate several beautiful Filipino wedding traditions:

The Veil Ceremony

A white veil is placed over the bride's head and the groom's shoulders, symbolizing their being clothed as one.

The Cord Ceremony

A decorative cord is draped in a figure-eight pattern around the couple's shoulders, symbolizing their eternal bond.

The Arrhae (Coin) Ceremony

The groom pours 13 coins into the bride's hands, symbolizing his dedication to his wife's well-being and their future family.
